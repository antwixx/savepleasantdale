Protect Pleasantdale

Defending our neighborhood, our history, and our future.

A Sanctuary, Not Just a Subdivision

Pleasantdale is a place where roots run deep. For nearly seven decades, our winding, tree-lined streets have been a sanctuary for families seeking a quieter life. It is a mosaic of generations—where grandparents who built their homes in the 1950s now wave to young children learning to ride their bikes on the very same sidewalks.

We are a community defined by connection, safety, and the peaceful stillness of nature. But today, an industrial shadow looms over our homes, threatening to unravel the fabric of our neighborhood forever.

Disrupting the Peace

A massive commercial development—a Gas N Wash truck stop—is proposed right at our doorstep. As illustrated, this project would forcefully insert heavy industry into a fragile residential ecosystem.

This is not just about zoning; it is about the encroachment of noise, pollution, and danger upon a community that was never designed to sustain it.

Protecting Nature

Preserving the delicate air and groundwater quality of the adjacent Waterfall Glen Forest Preserve from industrial runoff and fumes.

Safety First

Preventing 18-wheelers from flooding the local roads where our children wait for school buses and families walk their dogs.

Preserving Legacy

Stopping irreversible damage to the character of a historic neighborhood that has stood as a safe haven since 1957.
